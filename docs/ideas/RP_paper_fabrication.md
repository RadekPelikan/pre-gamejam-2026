### Lore

Create a game about agents operating at the heavily fortified border wall of a totalitarian regime. The outside world is ravaged by an analog horror apocalypse, but the regime's official stance is that everything is perfectly fine and utopian. 

You are the gatekeeper. Your job is to process people trying to enter, interrogate them, and fabricate reasons to deny entry to the horrors outside without ever breaking the state's illusion.

A core secret of the state is that some of the "people" outside are actually terrifying anomalies or monsters attempting to blend in. However, it is strictly illegal to acknowledge this. If a player ever refers to a "person" as a "monster," or calls these creatures by their true classified names, they face immediate punishment. 

I take heavy inspiration from papers please for game lore and style. [Papers please gameplay](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kxPKnDLR0rA&list=PLa2jLRLGuaM10kbv9-INqXLVSwKehQLRF)

I'd like to keep the entire lore and names fictional. Do not include any real names of parties, secret agencies or politicians.

### Gameplay

The core gameplay loop centers around interrogation, observation, and extreme paranoia. You are stationed in a claustrophobic checkpoint filled with surveillance cameras and regime wiretaps. 

* **Interrogating the "Outsiders":** People from the outside will approach your booth. Some are normal humans; others are poorly disguised anomalies. During the interrogation, these entities might slip up—they might say banned words, perform forbidden actions, or reveal physical glitches.
* **The Secret Book:** You are equipped with a highly classified, constantly updating rulebook. It contains the list of forbidden actions, subtle traits of the anomalies, and **a strict list of banned words—which explicitly includes the actual names and types of the monsters**. You must memorize and cross-reference this book constantly.
* **The Bureaucratic Trap:** If you spot an anomaly, you cannot sound a "monster alarm" (because monsters officially do not exist). Instead, you must find a mundane bureaucratic excuse to deny them entry (e.g., an expired permit, a misspelled name) to keep the regime's illusion intact.
* **Surveillance & Wiretapping:** The regime is always listening. If a player (via in-game text or voice chat) or an NPC says a banned word (like naming a specific monster type), it triggers an alert. If you fail to document and report an NPC who broke a rule, or if you break a rule yourself, you are penalized.
* **Multiplayer Paranoia:** Players work together in the checkpoint. Because of the wiretaps and the strict ban on mentioning monster types, players must invent their own code words to warn each other. At the end of a shift, a court session is held. If the team missed a banned action or let an anomaly through, the state might execute a random player or punish the entire team based on the severity of the mistake.
* **Custom Modes (Chaotic Fun):** Players can create custom regime rulesets (e.g., an "anti-brainrot" custom regime) where specific words or numbers (like typing "67") lead to an immediate, broadcasted execution.
